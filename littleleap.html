LittleLeap

Small but Meaningful Steps Forward

“Designing an inclusive digital companion for children with Down Syndrome and their caregivers.”
View Case Study Explore Screens

Overview

LittleLeap is an integrated learning and support system designed to help children with Down Syndrome develop essential skills through playful, accessible, and emotionally supportive experiences.

It also empowers parents with tools to track progress, manage routines, and support their child’s growth effortlessly.

The Problem

Children struggle with:

  • Communication and speech development
  • Maintaining routines
  • Cognitive overload from complex apps

Parents struggle with:

  • Lack of structured support tools
  • Difficulty tracking small progress
  • Scattered resources
Core Problem: There is no unified, child-friendly system that supports both learning and caregiving.

The Objective

Design a system that:

  • Enables learning through play
  • Supports emotional expression
  • Builds daily routines
  • Provides clear parent insights
  • Adapts to each child’s pace

Research Insights

Visual learning is most effective.

Gamification increases engagement.

Routine builds independence.

Parents need simplicity and clarity.

Emotional support is essential.

User Personas

Child

  • Loves colors, music, and games
  • Needs simple, visual interfaces
  • Gets overwhelmed easily

Parent

  • Needs simple management tools
  • Wants progress clarity
  • Seeks structured support

Empathy Mapping

The Solution

LittleLeap is a dual-system platform specifically created to bridge the gap between learning and caregiving.

Child App

  • Gamified learning
  • Emotion tools
  • Routine-based guidance
  • Smartwatch support

Parent Dashboard

  • Insights and tracking
  • Activity planning
  • Progress visualization

Design Walkthrough

A journey through the child and parent interfaces.

01. Calm & Breathing

Child's Tablet

Helps children regulate emotions through visual breathing guidance.

02. Emergency Help

Child's Tablet

Instant support reduces anxiety and builds safety when the child calls a parent.

03. Child Home

Child's Tablet

Simple, colorful navigation for easy, distraction-free interaction.

04. Child Profile

Child's Tablet

Personalized experience and avatar selection for each child.

05. Learning Section

Child's Tablet

Visual learning through engaging, accessible activities.

06. Play Section

Child's Tablet

Gamified learning drastically improves engagement and retention.

07. Emotion Selection

Child's Watch

Helps children identify and safely express their complex feelings.

08. Smartwatch Interface

Child's Watch

Quick-access support system right on the wrist for emergencies and routine reminders.

09. Parent Dashboard

Parent's Phone

A clear, reassuring overview of the child’s daily progress and status.

10. Insights Screen

Parent's Phone

Visual data simplifies understanding of long-term development metrics.

11. Device Linking

Parent's Phone

A seamless ecosystem connecting tablets, phones, and watches across devices.

12. Resources Section

Parent's Phone

Provides actionable guidance, community, and expert support for caregivers.

13. Routine Builder

Child's Tablet

Helps build structured, visual habits to increase independence and reduce friction.

Design Principles

  • Simplicity
  • Accessibility
  • Emotional comfort
  • Engagement through play
  • Consistency

Accessibility Core

  • Large touch targets
  • Clear typography
  • Soft colors to prevent overstimulation
  • Minimal cognitive load
  • Inclusive design

The Impact

For Children:

Improved learning

Better emotional expression

Increased independence

For Parents:

Reduced stress

Clear progress tracking

Better involvement

Key Learnings

  • Simplicity is powerful. A reduction in choices yields better focus.
  • Emotional design matters. Safe colors and microinteractions build trust.
  • Accessibility is essential. Designing for varied cognitive levels requires extreme empathy.
  • UX is about empathy. Understanding the daily stress of both parent and child changed everything.
